TEPCO is Japan's largest utility and one of the world's largest by capacity and revenue. Following the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster in March 2011, TEPCO underwent profound restructuring under government intervention, with the state acquiring a majority stake through the Nuclear Damage Compensation and Decommissioning Facilitation Corporation (NDF).

Corporate structure

TEPCO Holdings organises its operations into three pillars: Fuel & Power (wholesale generation and trading through JERA, a JV with Chubu Electric); TEPCO Power Grid (regulated transmission and distribution for Kanto); and Renewable Power (renewables development and investment).

Fukushima decommissioning

The Fukushima Daiichi decommissioning programme remains a multi-decade commitment. Estimated costs exceed ¥20 trillion. TEPCO works with the NDF and government on decommissioning progress. The treated water discharge programme began in August 2023 and continues under IAEA oversight.

Energy transition strategy

TEPCO's Zero Carbon 2050 Vision targets carbon neutrality. Near-term priorities include offshore wind development (targeting multi-GW in Tohoku and Kanto offshore zones), battery storage for grid stabilisation, and hydrogen/ammonia co-firing at thermal plants. The JERA joint venture with Chubu Electric is now one of Japan's largest power generators.
